Partisan Symmetry

Which districts would flip with a shift in support for Democrats?

-10% 0% +10%

Partisan symmetry doesn't expect perfectly proportional representation in Congress. It tests whether if the parties were to switch statewide vote totals, they would also switch seat totals.

In an ideal system, the parties would have equal representation at 50% and mirror each others achievements as their vote total rose. But here we can predict that Democrats would need 56% of the vote to win half of Pennsylvania's contested Congressional elections.
